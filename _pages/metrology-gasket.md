## Overview

This ME360 project involved manufacturing a **custom HDPE gasket** to fit a unique metal part. The full workflow spanned measurement, dimensioning, CAD design, and CNC milling.

---

## Requirements

The gasket was required to:
- Fit over two locating pins without obstruction
- Include four 10-32 clearance holes
- Clear two rectangular features on the mating part
- Achieve dimensional tolerance of **±0.005"**

---

## Manufacturing Process

1. **Measure** the metal part with calipers and dial indicators
2. **Model** the gasket geometry in CAD
3. **Generate** CNC toolpaths
4. **Mill** from HDPE stock on a CNC mill

---

## Challenges & Lessons Learned

Several measurement and precision errors were encountered:

- **Hole location errors:** Difficulty measuring clearance hole centers from the part edge
- **Pin diameter miscalculation:** Locating pin diameter measured too narrow, preventing proper fit
- **Feature positioning:** Rectangular feature locations placed too far from the edge
- **Measurement accuracy:** Misreading caliper and dial indicator values caused errors of ±0.1" — 20× larger than the required tolerance

**Key takeaway:** When manufacturing mating components to tight tolerances, measurement accuracy and process care are critical. The required ±0.005" tolerance leaves very little margin for error.

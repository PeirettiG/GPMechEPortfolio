## Overview

This ME360 project involved designing and prototyping a **handheld hot wire stamping/branding tool** that safely harnesses Nichrome wire heat while protecting the user. The tool needed electronic control capability and ergonomic functionality.

---

## Design Evolution

The team initially explored multiple concepts:

- **Hot blade design** — for cutting foam, styled like a pencil or scalpel with front-mounted control
- **Final selection: Stamping/branding tool** — held with a power grip, thumb-activated button

The stamping tool was selected because it offered a more ergonomic shape and an interesting use case.

---

## Technical Implementation

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Nichrome wire | Heating element |
| Copper wire | Electrical connections |
| Clay cement | Structural support & heat insulation |
| 3D-printed housing | Ergonomic handle form factor |

The clay cement served two purposes: providing structural support for the Nichrome wire connections, and insulating the 3D-printed housing from excessive heat transfer.

## Overview

This ME360 assignment required designing a **2.5 degree-of-freedom mechanism** controlled by a single actuator. The result is a centrifuge loader that dispenses metered amounts of material to a centrifuge used in chemistry labs.

---

## Design Concept

Rather than using Cartesian (X-Y) geometry, the mechanism uses **polar coordinates** — combining rotational movement with radial displacement. This allows a single linear actuator to address multiple dispensing positions.

### How It Works

1. A **rotating specimen plate** selects the target sample position
2. A **fixed linear drive arm** is positioned above the plate
3. Multiple dispensers are mounted at different radii
4. A **linear drive screw** provides radial movement to the arm
5. Individual dispensers activate as the arm reaches each position

---

## Design Evolution

The team initially conceived an accessory for standard centrifuges, but encountered challenges with stacking components vertically. The final design separates the selection (rotation) and dispensing (linear) functions onto different axes, simplifying the mechanism.

---

## Simulation

Due to software limitations in SolidWorks for simulating fluid dispensing, the team used a **small sphere as a proxy for fluid**, and the dispenser was designed to handle several spheres. SolidWorks motion studies validated the kinematic behavior.

---

## Development Status

The mechanism was completed as a **full CAD design with motion studies**. Physical construction was prevented by the COVID-19 pandemic, which closed the university machine shop during the Spring 2020 semester.
